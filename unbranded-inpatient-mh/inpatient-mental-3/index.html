Understanding Inpatient and Outpatient Treatment Options

  • Inpatient treatment: Designed for individuals requiring intensive care due to severe symptoms, safety concerns, or needing a structured environment for healing.

  • Provides ongoing support through individual or group therapy sessions, allowing for greater flexibility while managing mental health conditions.

Finding the Right Fit:
Inpatient vs. Outpatient Treatment

Feature Inpatient Treatment Outpatient Treatment
Level of Care Intensive 24/7 supervision and support Flexible therapy sessions with Varying frequency
Ideal for Severe symptoms, safety concerns, major life transitions Ongoing support, managing Chronic conditions, integrating therapy into daily life
Program Length Varies depending on individual needs Weekly or bi-weekly sessions; ongoing care as needed
Environment Structured and secure hospital setting Familiar surroundings (home, office)

Frequently Asked Questions

We understand you may have questions about our programs. Here are answers to some of the most common inquiries.

What insurance plans do you accept?
We accept a variety of insurance plans. Please contact our admissions team to verify coverage for your specific plan.
What are the qualifications of your therapists?
All our therapists are licensed mental health professionals with extensive experience treating various mental health conditions.
What happens during a consultation?
A consultation is a free and confidential meeting to discuss your needs and goals for treatment. We will assess your situation and recommend the most appropriate program for you.
Is medication-assisted treatment available?
Yes, medication-assisted treatment (MAT) may be integrated into your treatment plan if deemed necessary by a qualified healthcare professional.
What happens after I complete the program?
We offer aftercare planning to help you transition back to daily life and maintain your progress. This may include recommendations for ongoing therapy, support groups, or other resources.
