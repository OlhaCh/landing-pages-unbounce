Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What conditions do you treat?

We treat a variety of mental health conditions and substance use disorders, including depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, PTSD, and addiction.

Is family involvement part of the treatment?

The duration of treatment varies based on individual needs. Typically, residential programs can range from 30 to 90 days, with some individuals requiring longer stays depending on their progress and specific circumstances.

What is included in the treatment program?

Our treatment programs include a combination of individual therapy, group therapy, holistic therapies (such as yoga and meditation), medical support, family therapy, and aftercare planning to ensure lasting recovery.

How does the admissions process work?

The admissions process begins with a confidential assessment conducted by our admissions team. This helps us understand your unique needs and determine the most appropriate treatment plan. Once the assessment is complete, we guide you through the next steps, including insurance verification and scheduling your admission date.

Do you accept insurance?

Yes, we accept a variety of insurance plans. Our admissions team can help you verify your insurance coverage and discuss payment options. We also offer financial assistance for those who qualify.

What is a typical day like in the residential program?

A typical day includes structured therapy sessions, both individual and group, along with time for holistic activities such as exercise, art therapy, and relaxation techniques. Meals and recreational activities are also part of the daily schedule to ensure a balanced and supportive environment.

Is family involvement part of the treatment?

Yes, we believe family involvement is crucial for recovery. Our programs often include family therapy sessions to help repair and strengthen family relationships and provide education and support for family members.

What kind of support is available after I leave the program?

We offer comprehensive aftercare planning and support to help you transition back to daily life. This includes ongoing therapy, support groups, and resources to help you maintain your recovery and prevent relapse.

How do I know if residential treatment is right for me?

Residential treatment is typically recommended for individuals who need intensive, round-the-clock support in a structured environment. If you are struggling to manage your mental health or addiction issues on your own or through outpatient services, residential treatment might be the right choice for you..

How can I contact you if I have more questions?

You can reach out to us through our contact form, call our admissions hotline, or email us directly. We are here to answer any questions you may have and guide you through the process of getting the help you need.
